## SECTION 1: Vision & Big Picture

**1.1** In one sentence, what is FuelWell's core promise to a new user?

```
FuelWell is the AI nutrition coach that learns how you actually eat and live — so you stop guessing, stop counting, and start fueling your body right.
```

**1.2** If a viewer remembers only ONE thing after watching this video, what should it be?

```
"This app actually talks to me like a real coach — it knows my goals, my diet, my allergies, and gives me specific advice in plain English. It's not a spreadsheet. It's a conversation."

The takeaway is the AI COACHING CHAT — that's the emotional and functional differentiator. The moment a user sees someone ask the AI "I'm at a Mexican restaurant, what should I order?" and get a real, personalized, allergy-aware answer — that's the moment they download.
```

**1.3** What makes FuelWell fundamentally different from MyFitnessPal, Noom, Fitbod, and MacroFactor? Name the top 3 differentiators in order of importance.

```
1. CONVERSATIONAL AI COACHING — Not a calorie counter with a chatbot bolted on. FuelWell's AI coach knows your profile, macros, dietary preferences, allergies, and daily progress. You talk to it like a friend. No competitor does this natively — they either have no AI or use generic chatbots disconnected from your data.

2. ZERO FRICTION LOGGING — Three input modes (search, photo snap, barcode scan) with the meal type pre-selected. The goal is "log in under 10 seconds." MFP requires 4-5 taps minimum. FuelWell treats speed as a feature.

3. PERSONALIZATION ENGINE FROM DAY ONE — The 10-step onboarding wizard calculates your exact calorie/macro targets using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, factors in your activity level, goal (lose/maintain/gain), dietary preference (keto, vegan, etc.), and food allergies. The app is personalized before you even see the dashboard. Competitors ask 2-3 questions and give generic plans.
```

**1.4** What is the single feature or moment in the app that makes new users say "oh wow, this is different"? Describe exactly what they see and what triggers that reaction.

```
The "Oh Wow" moment happens on the AI Coach screen when a user types a real-world question and gets a response that KNOWS THEM.

Example: User types "I overate last night at a party — am I screwed?"
The AI responds: "Not at all! One night doesn't erase your progress. Based on your targets, here's how to adjust today: aim for about 1,600 calories instead of your usual 2,100, lean into protein-heavy meals, and skip the liquid calories. Want me to suggest a high-protein lunch that fits?"

The reaction trigger: The AI didn't judge. It didn't give a generic "eat less" response. It referenced THEIR specific calorie target, gave a concrete adjusted number, and offered to build them a specific meal. That's what a real human coach does. That's the moment.
```

**1.5** Is there a founding story, personal motivation, or "why we built this" narrative you want woven into the video? If so, describe it briefly.

```
Yes — but keep it subtle. Don't make it a founder spotlight.

The story: Max and Robby are two guys who got tired of nutrition apps that felt like spreadsheets. They both went through the cycle — download MFP, obsessively count calories for two weeks, burn out, delete the app, repeat. They realized the problem wasn't willpower — it was that the tools made healthy eating feel like homework.

How to weave it in: ONE line in the voiceover, around the empathy beat: "Built by two people who got tired of apps that felt like homework." That's it. Let the product speak. The story lives on the website, not the ad.
```

## SECTION 2: Target Audience & Emotional Tone

**2.1** Describe your ideal viewer of this video in detail — who are they, what's their day like, and what frustration are they feeling RIGHT before they find FuelWell?

```
PRIMARY VIEWER: "The Intentional Beginner"
- Age 25–38, working professional (remote or hybrid)
- Health-curious but not a gym rat — they want to eat better, not become a bodybuilder
- Has tried 2-4 fitness/nutrition apps in the past 2 years and abandoned all of them
- Their day: Wake up, skip breakfast or grab something random, work through lunch (desk meal), feel guilty about dinner choices, go to bed thinking "I'll start fresh Monday"
- The frustration: They KNOW what they should do generally, but they can't figure out what to eat specifically — for THEIR body, THEIR goals, THEIR dietary needs. Every app they've tried either over-simplifies (generic meal plans) or over-complicates (manually entering every gram of food)
- The emotional state: Fatigued by fitness culture. Tired of being lectured. Wants something that just WORKS without making them feel bad.

They're scrolling Instagram at 9pm. They see this ad. They're one thumb-tap from downloading another app — but they've been burned before. This video has 3 seconds to prove FuelWell is different.
```

**2.2** Are there secondary audiences the video should also resonate with? (e.g., adaptive athletes, postpartum fitness, seniors, people managing chronic conditions)

```
Yes — and this matters for both the video content AND the representation in lifestyle footage:

1. PEOPLE WITH DIETARY RESTRICTIONS — Vegans, vegetarians, keto followers, people with food allergies. FuelWell's onboarding captures all of this. Show a moment where the AI coach factors in allergies or dietary preferences — this is a massive underserved market.

2. AGE DIVERSITY — Don't just show 25-year-old influencer types. Include someone 40-55 checking their meal plan at a kitchen counter. Nutrition coaching is MORE valuable as metabolism changes with age.

3. ADAPTIVE ATHLETES — FuelWell's activity level selector already accommodates different levels. A brief shot of a wheelchair athlete or someone with a physical difference using the app sends a powerful brand signal without being performative. It says: "this app is for every body."

4. POSTPARTUM / BODY RECOMPOSITION — People whose bodies have changed and who need to rebuild their relationship with food. FuelWell's non-judgmental AI tone ("not screwed, just adjust") is perfect for this audience.

Do NOT make secondary audiences the focus — they should be woven into the lifestyle B-roll naturally. The primary audience is still the time-poor professional.
```

**2.3** What emotional journey should the viewer experience from second 1 to the final frame?

```
Your preferred order (top = first emotion, bottom = last):
1. Frustrated / seen ("that's me") — Hook + pain point (0-8s)
2. Curious ("wait, what is this?") — FuelWell reveal (8-15s)
3. Impressed ("that's smart") — AI Coach showcase (15-30s)
4. Trusting ("this seems legit") — Dashboard + real data (30-42s)
5. Empowered ("I can do this") — CTA + closing (42-60s)

NOTE: I dropped "Urgent" entirely. FuelWell's brand is NOT about urgency or scarcity. It's about empowerment. The viewer should feel "I want this because it makes me feel capable" — not "I need this because I'll miss out." Urgency is for supplement brands, not coaching brands.
```

**2.4** What tone should the voiceover NOT sound like?

```
- NOT like a CrossFit bro yelling about gains
- NOT like a weight loss infomercial ("Lose 30 pounds in 30 days!")
- NOT like a clinical dietitian reading from a textbook
- NOT like a condescending wellness influencer ("you just need to love yourself more")
- NOT like a tech demo ("leveraging our proprietary AI neural network pipeline")
- NOT like a sad documentary about obesity

It SHOULD sound like: Your smart friend who happens to know a lot about nutrition, casually explaining something cool they found. Confident but not arrogant. Warm but not soft. Direct but not pushy. Think: the tone of a Calm app ad meets the energy of an Apple product reveal.
```

## SECTION 6: The Hook (First 3 Seconds)

**6.1** Hook strategy ranking:

```
Ranking:
1. Pain point hook — "You've downloaded 6 fitness apps this year. Deleted 5."
2. Question hook — "What if your phone actually knew what your body needs?"
3. Demo hook — Jump straight into the app doing something impressive (no VO)
4. Stat hook — "87% of fitness apps get abandoned in the first week."
5. Bold claim — "The last fitness app you'll ever download."

WHY #1 WINS: It's specific, relatable, and slightly confrontational in a way that earns attention without being aggressive. Everyone who's tried fitness apps has this exact experience. It makes them feel SEEN — which is the first emotion in our arc (Section 2.3). The number "6" is specific enough to feel real, "deleted 5" has a punchline quality. It also naturally sets up the next beat: "So what makes this one different?"

Bold claim is LAST because it's the kind of thing every app says. It's generic and invites skepticism.
```

**6.2** Custom hook option:

```
"You don't need another calorie counter. You need a coach."

This hook works because:
- It immediately disqualifies the competition (MFP, Lose It, etc. — they're "calorie counters")
- It elevates FuelWell to a different category ("coach")
- It's 9 words — punchy enough for 2 seconds
- It naturally leads into the FuelWell reveal

A/B TEST ALL THREE: Pain point hook, question hook, and this custom hook. The data will tell us which converts best.
```

```

## SECTION 10: Final Gut Check

**10.1** If this video is a 10/10 success, what happens?

```
Three things happen:

1. CONVERSION: A health-conscious professional watches the video, sees themselves in the "pain point" beat, is genuinely impressed by the AI Coach interaction, and downloads the app within 60 seconds. They complete onboarding and log their first meal the same day. Cost-per-install under $3 on Meta ads.

2. DIFFERENTIATION: When someone asks "how is FuelWell different from MyFitnessPal?" — they can answer in one sentence because the video made it crystal clear: "It's a coach, not a counter." The video creates a new category in the viewer's mind.

3. VIRALITY SEED: The AI Coach chat moment is so striking that people screenshot it and share it in group chats, fitness forums, or Instagram stories. "Have you seen this app?" organic word-of-mouth. The video gets shared not because it's a great AD, but because it shows a genuinely impressive PRODUCT MOMENT.
```

**10.2** If this video fails, what went wrong?

```
The nightmare scenario:

1. IT LOOKS LIKE EVERY OTHER FITNESS APP AD — Generic stock footage of someone jogging, close-up of a salad, generic "get healthy" messaging.
